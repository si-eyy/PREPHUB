What is a Disaster?

A disaster is a sudden or gradual event that causes serious disruption to a community, environment, or country. It leads to widespread damage, loss of life, destruction of property, and interruption of everyday activities. Disasters overwhelm the ability of people and local authorities to cope using their usual resources.

Disasters can be natural (typhoons, earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, floods, tsunamis) or human-made (fires, industrial accidents, chemical spills, pollution).

What To Do

Before a Disaster

  • Identify hazards common in your area
  • Create and practice a family emergency plan
  • Prepare an emergency kit with essentials
  • Store important documents in waterproof containers
  • Know nearest evacuation centers and hospitals
  • Learn basic first aid and CPR
  • Secure your home (fix roofs, windows, electrical)
  • Keep drainage systems clean
  • Save emergency hotlines
  • Stay updated with weather forecasts from PAGASA

During a Disaster

  • Stay calm and avoid panicking
  • Follow evacuation orders immediately
  • Keep emergency kit close
  • Assist vulnerable family members
  • Move to higher ground during floods
  • Stay away from windows and falling objects
  • Turn off electricity to prevent fire
  • Use flashlight, never candles
  • Stay tuned to radio and official announcements
  • If trapped, signal for help with whistle or flashlight

After a Disaster

  • Check yourself and family for injuries
  • Wait for official clearance before returning home
  • Inspect house for structural damage
  • Stay away from fallen power lines
  • Photograph damages for insurance
  • Clean up debris carefully with protective gear
  • Boil water before drinking if supply is uncertain
  • Help neighbors, especially elderly or disabled
  • Monitor physical and mental health
  • Restock emergency kit for future preparedness

Basic First Aid Principles

1. Ensure Safety

Assess scene for dangers. Make sure it's safe before approaching.

2. Assess Situation

Check consciousness. Observe for life-threatening conditions.

3. Call for Help

Dial emergency number. Provide clear location and injury information.

4. ABC Check

Airway, Breathing, Circulation. Begin CPR if needed.

5. Control Bleeding

Apply firm pressure with clean cloth. Keep injured area elevated.

6. Treat for Shock

Lay person down, elevate legs. Keep warm and reassure.
